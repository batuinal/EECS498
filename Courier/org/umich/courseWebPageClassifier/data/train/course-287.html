Course Description
  Top

In quantum mechanics, the Schrodinger equation is a partial differential equation that describes how the quantum state of some physical system changes with time. In this course we will discuss the mathematics behind the Schrodinger operator. Topics to be covered include: self-adjoint operators, quadratic forms, variational principle, spectral theorem, discrete and essential spectrum. We illustrate these abstract concepts by the concrete examples of Schrodinger operators. In this course we will mainly focus on the discrete spectrum and discuss also Birman-Schwinger principle, Sobolev inequalities, Hardy inequalities, Lieb-Thirring inequalities, uniqueness of the ground state, decay of eigenfunctions, etc.
