COURSE DESCRIPTION

This course presents an introduction to the heat equation proof of the Atiyah-Singer index theorem and index theory in noncommutative geometry. In the noncommutative setting, we will emphasize on Connes' index formula: the analytic index, given by pairing a K-homology and a K-theory element of a noncommutative algebra, coincides with the topological index, which pairs the corresponding elements in periodic cyclic cohomology and homology obtained by the Connes-Chern characters.

PREREQUISITES

An acquaintance with manifolds, exterior derivative, Riemannian metrics, spectral theorem for compact self-adjoint operators on a Hilbert space, and \(C^* \)-algebras will be very useful.

POLICIES

The evaluation will be based on class participation and a final presentation for each student. The topics will be assigned to the students according to their interests from research articles and books that are related to the topics covered in the course. A list of papers suitable for this purpose will be posted on the course webpage in March 2015, and more papers will be added to the list during the Spring quarter.

TOPICS COVERED

Fourier transform, pseudodifferential calculus, Fredholm operators, heat kernel method for computing the Seeley-de Witt coefficients and the index of an elliptic operator on a closed manifold, Hodge theorem, characteristic classes, Dirac operator, Atiyah-Singer index theorem, examples of noncommutative geometric spaces, cyclic cohomology, Fredholm modules, Connes-Chern character, Connes' index formula, a brief outline of the local index formula of Connes and Moscovici for spectral triples and their work on preparing the ground for extending the formula to twisted spectral triples (which is still open).
