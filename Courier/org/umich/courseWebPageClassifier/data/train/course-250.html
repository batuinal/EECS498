Textbook:  The class will not follow closely any textbook (but I will keep posting my lecture notes). If you want to purchase a textbook (not required if you can keep up with the pace of the class), the following one is well written and, compared to Boyce and DiPrima, has a stronger emphasis on linear algebra and nonlinear ODEs. If you already have Boyce and DiPrima, you do not need to purchase another one.

  • Michael E. Taylor; Introduction to Differential Equations.

Optional Textbooks:  

  • V.I. Arnold. Ordinary differential equations (this is a classic and one of the most beautiful books written on the subject of ODEs and dynamical systems, presents deep geometric aspects at a level accessible to undergraduate students).
  • Boyce and DiPrima; Elementary Differential Equations.  (Taylor has a stronger emphasis on linear algebra and nonlinear ODEs).
  • Paul Dawkins’ lecture notes on Ordinary Differential Equations. Can be downloaded at http://tutorial.math.lamar.edu/pdf/DE/DE_Complete.pdf (could be useful if you are struggling with basic concepts).
  • Paul Dawkins’ lecture notes on linear algebra. Available at http://tutorial.math.lamar.edu/ (could be useful if you are struggling with basic concepts).
  • J.C. Butcher; Numerical Methods for Ordinary Differential Equations, (one of the most comprehensive references on numerical methods for ODEs, very well written and useful beyond the scope of the class).
  • J. D. Hoffmann. Numerical Methods for Engineers and Scientists (reads like a novel, you may like the style, useful beyond the scope of the class).
  • R. S. Palais and R.  A. Palais. Differential Equations, Mechanics, and Computation.
  • Tony Lelievre’s lecture notes on Ordinary Differential Equations (very good but in French, http://cermics.enpc.fr/~lelievre/MOPSI/MOPSI_EDO.pdf)
  • E. Hairer’s lecture notes on numerical methods, ordinary differential equations and geometric integration (comprehensive and well written, some are in French http://www.unige.ch/~hairer/polycop.html).
  • J. Frank’s lecture notes on Numerical Modeling of Dynamical Systems (quite good, http://homepages.cwi.nl/~jason/Classes/numwisk/index.html)
  • J. R. Chasnov’ lecture notes on ODEs (follows Boyce and DiPrima, http://www.math.ust.hk/~machas/differential-equations.pdf)
