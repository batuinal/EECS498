ANNOUNCEMENTS

 

COURSE DESCRIPTION

This course will introduce the students to the wonderful world of Numbers, which play an increasing role in a variety of things including codes and cryptography. Previous knowledge of this field is not necessary, only curiosity and attention to detail. The lectures will cover roughly the same material as in the text, but will often have a different emphasis and presentation. Supplementary information will be provided in the online notes by the Instructor.

All are welcome, including freshmen.

It will be useful for the students to experiment with PARI, which is a useful source for computational power specifically geared towards solving problems arising in Number Theory. This has now been incorporated in Sage (see the Intro and QuickReference in the Handouts below).

Here is an explicit list of topics which will be discussed:  Primes, divisibility, Pythagorean triples, congruences, Euler's phi-function, solutions modulo a prime, Fermat, Mersenne primes, RSA encryption, Quadratic reciprocity, sums of squares, Gaussian integers, and diophantine approximations.

PREREQUISITES

POLICIES

Homework Policy

Working together on homework is permitted but each student must write up their own solution in their own words. Do not consult solution sets from previous years.

The course grade will depend on both a final collaborative, computational project and on the homework scores.
