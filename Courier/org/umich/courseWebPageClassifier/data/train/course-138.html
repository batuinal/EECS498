About The Course

MWF 1:00 - 1:50, CIT 368

Welcome to CS1951-E. This course explores topics in computer systems security. It provides a concrete introduction to cryptography; hardware, software, network and web security; and non-computer aspects of computer security. It also introduces principles designed to anticipate, assess and minimize security risks and examines a variety of techniques for passive and active defense and defense in depth. Finally, it explores the current security landscape and examines social issues relevant to security such as censorship and the growing threat of large-scale cyber attacks.

This course aims to balance theory and practice. There are weekly homeworks, tri-weekly blog posts, and four hands-on projects. Homeworks will ask you to think critically about security questions. Blog posts will ask you to examine the world around you with the eye of a security professional or attacker. Projects will ask you to get down into the mud and do some making and breaking.
